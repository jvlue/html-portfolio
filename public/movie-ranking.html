Top Underrated Movies of All Time

My Top 3 Underrated Movies

1. Olympus has Fallen

Olympus Has Fallen (2013) is an action-thriller directed by Antoine Fuqua, starring Gerard Butler, Aaron Eckhart, Morgan Freeman, and Angela Bassett. The film centers on a high-stakes hostage crisis at the White House, also known as "Olympus" in the film, after it is attacked by a North Korean terrorist group.

Plot Summary:

The story follows Mike Banning (played by Gerard Butler), a former U.S. Secret Service agent who is assigned to protect President Benjamin Asher (Aaron Eckhart). After an attack on the White House by a group of North Korean terrorists, the President is taken hostage along with other key figures in the government.

Banning, who had previously been demoted following a personal tragedy, finds himself in the right place at the right time and is the only one capable of stopping the terrorists from carrying out their plan. With the nation’s fate hanging in the balance, Banning uses his military training and inside knowledge of the White House to fight back, trying to rescue the President and prevent a larger-scale disaster.

Main Cast:

  • Gerard Butler as Mike Banning, the protagonist and ex-Secret Service agent.
  • Aaron Eckhart as President Benjamin Asher, who is taken hostage.
  • Morgan Freeman as Speaker of the House Allan Trumbull, who is tasked with leading the country during the crisis.
  • Angela Bassett as Secret Service Director Lynne Jacobs.
  • Rick Yune as Kang Yeonsak, the North Korean mastermind behind the attack.

2. Titanic

Titanic (1997) is an epic romance and disaster film directed, written, co-produced, and co-edited by James Cameron. It tells the fictionalized story of a young couple who fall in love aboard the ill-fated RMS Titanic during its maiden voyage in 1912. The film became one of the most iconic and commercially successful movies of all time.

Plot Summary:

The movie is framed around a modern-day search for treasure at the wreckage site of the Titanic. A treasure hunter named Brock Lovett (played by Bill Paxton) and his team discover a priceless necklace known as "The Heart of the Ocean" in the wreckage. This discovery leads to an elderly woman, Rose Dawson Calvert (played by Gloria Stuart), coming forward to recount her experience aboard the Titanic as a young woman.

The bulk of the movie takes place in flashback, focusing on Rose DeWitt Bukater (played by Kate Winslet) as a 17-year-old woman from a wealthy family, and Jack Dawson (played by Leonardo DiCaprio), a penniless artist, whom she meets aboard the ship. Despite the class divide and Rose's engagement to the wealthy but controlling Cal Hockley (played by Billy Zane), she forms a passionate and tragic love affair with Jack.

Their romance unfolds against the backdrop of the Titanic's fateful voyage, which tragically ends when the ship strikes an iceberg and sinks into the Atlantic Ocean. As the ship goes down, the film showcases moments of intense heroism, sacrifice, and chaos. Jack and Rose fight to survive, but ultimately, Jack dies in the cold waters of the ocean, leaving Rose to carry on with her life.

The movie ends with an older Rose throwing the Heart of the Ocean necklace into the sea, signifying her final release from the past and her reconnection to Jack, whom she never forgot.

Main Cast:

  • Leonardo DiCaprio as Jack Dawson, a poor artist and free spirit who falls in love with Rose.
  • Kate Winslet as Rose DeWitt Bukater, a young woman trapped by societal expectations who finds freedom and love in Jack.
  • Billy Zane as Caledon "Cal" Hockley, Rose's wealthy, arrogant fiancé.
  • Danny Nucci as Fabrizio, Jack's Italian friend and fellow passenger.
  • Gloria Stuart as the elderly Rose Dawson Calvert.

3. Oblivion

Oblivion (2013) is a science fiction film directed by Joseph Kosinski and starring Tom Cruise, Morgan Freeman, and Andrea Riseborough. It blends elements of post-apocalyptic survival with intriguing mysteries about identity and humanity. Here's a breakdown of the film:

Plot Summary:

The film is set in 2077, years after Earth was devastated by an alien invasion. Most of humanity has fled the planet, leaving behind a small population of survivors who live on Titan, one of Saturn's moons. Earth is now largely uninhabitable, with a barren landscape and remnants of human civilization destroyed by the alien race known as the Scavengers.

Jack Harper (played by Tom Cruise) is a technician stationed in a remote area of the Earth, tasked with maintaining and repairing drones that protect vital resources from the remaining Scavengers. Jack works with his partner Victoria (Andrea Riseborough), who communicates with him from a high-tech base in the sky. Their mission is to collect the last remaining resources to send back to the humans living on Titan.

However, Jack begins to have strange dreams about a woman, Julia (Olga Kurylenko), and a crash he witnessed years earlier, which leads him to question the true nature of his mission. When he encounters a spacecraft that crash-lands on Earth, he finds Julia alive—she claims to have known him before, although he has no memory of her.

As Jack uncovers more secrets about the past, he discovers that things are not as they seem. The Scavengers are not what they were thought to be, and the true history of Earth's fall and humanity's survival is far more complex than he imagined. Jack must navigate the mysteries of his own identity, uncover the truth behind the invasion, and ultimately decide what to do with the knowledge he has uncovered.

Main Cast:

  • Tom Cruise as Jack Harper, a technician who works on Earth’s surface after the alien invasion.
  • Andrea Riseborough as Victoria, Jack’s partner and mission control operator.
  • Morgan Freeman as Malcolm Beech, a resistance leader who has lived in hiding and knows the truth about Earth's fate.
  • Olga Kurylenko as Julia Rusakova, a mysterious woman from Jack's past.
  • Nikolaj Coster-Waldau as Sykes, a member of the human resistance.
